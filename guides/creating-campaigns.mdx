# Creating Campaigns

A campaign is a one-time email or SMS send to an explicit audience.

## Steps

1. **Choose a name** — for internal tracking
2. **Select a template** — pick an existing template or create one inline
3. **Pick your audience** — choose contact lists, a segment, or All Contacts
4. **Preview and test** — send a test email to yourself
5. **Send or schedule** — send immediately or pick a date and time

## Audience options

| Audience | Use when |
|----------|----------|
| Contact lists | You want to send to one or more curated lists. Lists can also be filtered by a segment. |
| Segment | You want to send to contacts matching saved rules, without choosing a list first. |
| All Contacts | You intentionally want to send to every subscribed contact in the brand. |

If no audience is selected, the campaign stays a draft and delivery is blocked. All Contacts requires a confirmation before sending or scheduling.

## Campaign states

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Draft | Being edited, not yet sent |
| Scheduled | Queued for a future send time |
| Sending | Currently being delivered |
| Sent | Delivery complete |

## Tracking

After sending, view open rates, click rates, and bounce/unsubscribe metrics on the campaign detail page.

## Iterate on a sent campaign

Sent campaigns are locked — their content, audience, schedule, and template can't be changed so the record stays faithful to what went out. To send a variant, click **Edit as new campaign** on the locked banner (or **Duplicate** from the overflow menu on any row). You get a fresh draft that inherits the audience and email but resets the schedule and approval, ready for you to tweak and re-send.

Renaming a sent campaign still works on any status — the name is internal metadata.

See [Campaigns → Edit lock and lifecycle](/concepts/campaigns#edit-lock-and-lifecycle) for the full matrix.
